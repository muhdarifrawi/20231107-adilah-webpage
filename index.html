Design Competition: Celebration of our Cultures

DIMENSION

There was a bunch of ideas that can be branched out from this topic such as the food culture, festivals commonly celebrated, the music culture. However, these ideas are also too vague and can be lengthy. Since Singapore is a diverse community with different cultures and religion, it can be tedious to talk about the common food or festivals.

I have chosen music as my design process. Music comes in various forms, however, I wanted to talk more of the classics as well as tradition. In Singapore, when we talk about classical, it has always been the Symphony Orchestra. It consists of the western instruments such as the harp, tuba, trombone, cello, violin and few others. South Korea, on the other hand, has their own traditional music, Jeongak, which means “right (or proper) music”. This includes both instrumental and vocal music which were cultivated mainly by the upper class of Joseon society. Instruments include janggu, haegum, piri, gayageum, buk.

Overall, the composition of the final design is decided after multiple consideration of sizes and placements that would allow people to view the few different types of instruments these two countries incorporate in their classical or traditional music.

Title: The Forgotten world

DIMENSION

This work is set in the future. With advancement of technologies, the older ones get discarded rather quick. I illustrated the cat with a half and half robotic parts and the skin of it to show that it has been there for years/ decades yet remains steady/ carefree. The tree represents the peaceful and quiet environment in the junkyard. Although it might have been run down, the tree remains strong. The boBom part of the art where there are fishes represents the tranquillity of the area. It is depicted in full white to indicate that those are the souls of the fishes who had passed in the making of a tech world. For the mediums/techniques I used for this work. The cat and the bark of the tree are done using the hatching/cross-hatching in pens and the rest are done using the mark making technique in Chinese ink.
